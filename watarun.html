WAT ARUN

Highlight: Wat Arun is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful riverside temples in Thailand and an iconic landmark of Bangkok. Situated along the banks of the Chao Phraya River, the temple is especially breathtaking during sunset, when the warm golden light reflects off the water and illuminates the temple’s intricate surfaces, creating a magical and unforgettable atmosphere. Also known as the “Temple of Dawn,” Wat Arun is most famous for its towering central prang, which rises approximately 70 meters high. This Khmer-style tower is richly decorated with colorful porcelain fragments, seashells, and delicate patterns that sparkle under sunlight and glow softly in the evening. Surrounding the main prang are smaller satellite towers, all contributing to a harmonious and visually striking composition that represents Mount Meru, the center of the universe in Buddhist cosmology. The temple is not only admired for its architectural beauty but also for its historical and cultural significance. It has played an important role since the Ayutthaya period and later became a royal temple during the early Rattanakosin era. Visitors can climb the steep steps of the central prang to enjoy panoramic views of the Chao Phraya River and the surrounding cityscape, including a stunning view of The Grand Palace on the opposite side. With its unique riverside setting, remarkable design, and captivating atmosphere at different times of the day—especially at sunset—Wat Arun offers an unforgettable cultural experience and stands as one of the most photogenic and treasured landmarks in Thailand.
