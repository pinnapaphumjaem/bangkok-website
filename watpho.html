WAT PHO

Highlight: Wat Pho is one of the oldest and largest temples in Bangkok, renowned worldwide for its magnificent Reclining Buddha and its role as a center of traditional Thai knowledge. The temple is home to the iconic Reclining Buddha statue, which measures approximately 46 meters in length and 15 meters in height, making it one of the largest and most impressive Buddha images in Thailand. Covered in gold leaf, the statue represents the Buddha entering Nirvana, symbolizing peace, enlightenment, and the end of all suffering. One of the most remarkable features of the statue is its feet, which are inlaid with intricate mother-of-pearl designs depicting 108 auspicious symbols of the Buddha. Surrounding the statue, visitors can also experience a unique cultural tradition by dropping coins into a row of bronze bowls, believed to bring good fortune and support temple maintenance. Beyond the Reclining Buddha, Wat Pho is also considered the birthplace of traditional Thai massage and serves as a leading center for Thai medicine and wellness education. The temple complex is filled with beautifully decorated chedis, serene courtyards, and detailed murals, reflecting the rich artistic and cultural heritage of Thailand. Its peaceful atmosphere combined with its historical significance makes Wat Pho a must-visit destination for those seeking both cultural insight and spiritual experience.
